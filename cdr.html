Crash Data Retrieval (CDR)

CDR is a tool used to retrieve data from a vehicle’s Event Data Recorder (EDR) which is part of the Airbag Control Module (ACM). Seconds before, during and after a crash event, data such as vehicle speed (MPH), engine speed (RPM), delta-v, and seat belt usage can be stored. This technology has been in existence since the 1990’s for GM and Ford vehicles but the data was very limited. Vetronix Corporation originally developed the CDR tool but now it is built by BOSCH. Today, the majority of GM, Ford, Chrysler, and some foreign auto manufactures have the capabilities to record critical vehicle systems and performance information. This accurate crash data though not intended to replace traditional reconstruction methodology may be used to support or refute other evidence in your particular case.

See below for possible data recorded:

  • Speed before impact
  • Engine speed (RPM)
  • Delta - V
  • Seat belt usage
  • Seat positioning
  • Ignition cycle at impact or at retrieval
  • Steering Input
  • Brakes engaged
  • Anti-lock brake engaged
  • Other vehicle system and performance information

At Collision Analysis Group, staff are certified CDR Technicians and CDR Analyst trained by BOSCH lead instructors. You can feel confident that this critical information will be obtained properly following protocol.

Clients will receive the data and graphs from the download. In addition, an analysis of the data and a written report explaining the data will be provided.
